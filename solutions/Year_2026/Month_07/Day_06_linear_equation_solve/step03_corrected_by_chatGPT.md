# Solve the Equation

## Problem

Solve the equation

$$
x+5=14-\frac{1}{2}x
$$

## Notes

1. To solve the equation means to find $x$ such that it satisfies the relationship

$$
x+5=14-\frac{1}{2}x.
$$

2. Start from the original equation.

$$
\begin{aligned}
x+5 &= 14-\frac{1}{2}x
&& [\text{original equation}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
x+5+\frac{1}{2}x
&=
14-\frac{1}{2}x+\frac{1}{2}x
&& [\because \text{addition property of equality}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
x+5+\frac{1}{2}x
&=
14+\left(-\frac{1}{2}x+\frac{1}{2}x\right)
&& [\because \text{associativity}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
x+5+\frac{1}{2}x
&=
14+0
&& [\because \text{additive inverse property}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
x+5+\frac{1}{2}x
&=
14
&& [\because \text{additive identity property}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
x+\frac{1}{2}x+5
&=
14
&& [\because \text{commutativity}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
\frac{3}{2}x+5
&=
14
&& [\because \text{combine like terms}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
\frac{3}{2}x+(5-5)
&=
14-5
&& [\because \text{subtraction property of equality}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
\frac{3}{2}x+0
&=
9
&& [\because \text{additive inverse property}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
\frac{3}{2}x
&=
9
&& [\because \text{additive identity property}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
2\cdot\frac{3}{2}x
&=
2\cdot 9
&& [\because \text{multiplication property of equality}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
3x
&=
18
&& [\because \text{associativity}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
\frac{1}{3}\cdot 3x
&=
\frac{1}{3}\cdot 18
&& [\because \text{multiplication property of equality}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
\left(\frac{1}{3}\cdot 3\right)x
&=
6
&& [\because \text{associativity}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
1x
&=
6
&& [\because \text{multiplicative inverse property}] \\[6pt]

\Rightarrow\quad
x
&=
6
&& [\because \text{multiplicative identity property}]
\end{aligned}
$$

## Answer

$$
\boxed{x=6}
$$
