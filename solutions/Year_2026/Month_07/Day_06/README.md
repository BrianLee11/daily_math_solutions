# Solve the Equation

## Problem

Solve the equation

$$
x+5=14-\frac{1}{2}x
$$


## Answer

$$
\boxed{x=6}
$$


## Explanations

1. To solve the equation means to find $x$ such that it satisfies the relationship

$$
x+5=14-\frac{1}{2}x.
$$

2. Start from the original equation.

$$
\begin{aligned}
x+5 &= 14-\frac{1}{2}x
&& [\text{original equation}] \\
x+5+\frac{1}{2}x
&= 14-\frac{1}{2}x+\frac{1}{2}x
&& [\because \text{addition property of equality}] \\
x+5+\frac{1}{2}x
&= 14+\left(-\frac{1}{2}x+\frac{1}{2}x\right)
&& [\because \text{associativity}] \\
x+5+\frac{1}{2}x
&= 14+0
&& [\because \text{additive inverse property}] \\
x+5+\frac{1}{2}x
&= 14
&& [\because \text{additive identity property}] \\
x+\frac{1}{2}x+5
&= 14
&& [\because \text{commutativity}] \\
\frac{3}{2}x+5
&= 14
&& [\because \text{combine like terms}] \\
\frac{3}{2}x+(5-5)
&= 14-5
&& [\because \text{subtraction property of equality}] \\
\frac{3}{2}x+0
&= 9
&& [\because \text{additive inverse property}] \\
\frac{3}{2}x
&= 9
&& [\because \text{additive identity property}] \\
2\cdot\frac{3}{2}x
&= 2\cdot 9
&& [\because \text{multiplication property of equality}] \\
3x
&= 18
&& [\because \text{associativity}] \\
\frac{1}{3}\cdot 3x
&= \frac{1}{3}\cdot 18
&& [\because \text{multiplication property of equality}] \\
\left(\frac{1}{3}\cdot 3\right)x
&= 6
&& [\because \text{associativity}] \\
1x
&= 6
&& [\because \text{multiplicative inverse property}] \\
x
&= 6
&& [\because \text{multiplicative identity property}]
\end{aligned}
$$

## Answer

$$
\boxed{x=6}
$$
