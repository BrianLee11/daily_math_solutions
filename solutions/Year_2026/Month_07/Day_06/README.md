# Solve the Equation

## Problem

Solve the equation

$$
x+5=14-\frac{1}{2}x
$$


## Answer

$$
\boxed{x=6}
$$

## Explanations

$$
\begin{array}{r r c l l}
& x+5 & = & 14-\frac{1}{2}x & [\text{original equation}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & x+5+\frac{1}{2}x & = & 14-\frac{1}{2}x+\frac{1}{2}x & [\because \text{addition property of equality}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & x+5+\frac{1}{2}x & = & 14+\left(-\frac{1}{2}x+\frac{1}{2}x\right) & [\because \text{associativity}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & x+5+\frac{1}{2}x & = & 14+0 & [\because \text{additive inverse property}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & x+5+\frac{1}{2}x & = & 14 & [\because \text{additive identity property}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & x+\frac{1}{2}x+5 & = & 14 & [\because \text{commutativity}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & \frac{3}{2}x+5 & = & 14 & [\because \text{combine like terms}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & \frac{3}{2}x+(5-5) & = & 14-5 & [\because \text{subtraction property of equality}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & \frac{3}{2}x+0 & = & 9 & [\because \text{additive inverse property}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & \frac{3}{2}x & = & 9 & [\because \text{additive identity property}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & 2\cdot\frac{3}{2}x & = & 2\cdot 9 & [\because \text{multiplication property of equality}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & 3x & = & 18 & [\because \text{associativity}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & \frac{1}{3}\cdot 3x & = & \frac{1}{3}\cdot 18 & [\because \text{multiplication property of equality}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & \left(\frac{1}{3}\cdot 3\right)x & = & 6 & [\because \text{associativity}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & 1x & = & 6 & [\because \text{multiplicative inverse property}] \\[6pt]
\Rightarrow & x & = & 6 & [\because \text{multiplicative identity property}]
\end{array}
$$

## Answer

$$
\boxed{x=6}
$$
