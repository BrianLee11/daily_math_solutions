Rewrite by completing the square. original equation: $x^{2}+x+1$

Note: complete the square means: find constants $b, c$ such that

$$
x^{2}+x+1=(x+b)^{2}+c
$$

$$
\Leftrightarrow x^{2}+2 x b+b^{2}+c
$$

$\Leftrightarrow 2 b=1$ and $b^{2}+c=1$
$\Rightarrow b=\frac{1}{2}$ and $\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{2}+c=1$
$\Rightarrow b=\frac{1}{2}$ and $c=1-\frac{1}{4}=\frac{3}{4}$
$\Rightarrow x^{2}+x+1=\left(x+\frac{1}{2}\right)^{2}+\frac{3}{4}$
$\therefore\left(x+\frac{1}{2}\right)^{2}+\frac{3}{4}$
